# RmFindr 2-Minute Demo Video Script

## Purpose

Show the product working. This is the "look, it moves" video.

## Structure

### 0:00-0:10 - Hook

Visual: person enters a building or stands at a doorway; quick cut to dashboard.

Voiceover:
"GPS can get you to the building. RmFindr helps once you are inside."

On-screen text:
"Indoor positioning using Wi-Fi fingerprints"

### 0:10-0:25 - Setup

Visual: three phone hotspots placed around the room, labeled Anchor A, Anchor B, Anchor C.

Voiceover:
"For this hackathon prototype, three phones act as fixed Wi-Fi anchors. A laptop scans their signal strengths as it moves through the room."

### 0:25-0:45 - Calibration

Visual: dashboard floor plan; user clicks known points; RSSI values appear and are saved.

Voiceover:
"First, we map the room. At each known location, RmFindr saves the Wi-Fi signal pattern. These patterns become the fingerprint map."

Suggested screen emphasis:
- Anchor markers
- Saved calibration points
- One visible RSSI reading, such as Anchor A -51, Anchor B -67, Anchor C -74

### 0:45-1:20 - Live Tracking

Visual: laptop moves to two or three locations; dashboard dot updates; trail appears.

Voiceover:
"During live tracking, the laptop reads current Wi-Fi signal strengths. The backend compares the live pattern against the saved fingerprints and estimates the current position."

Suggested cuts:
- Start position
- Move across room
- Dot updates
- Recent movement trail

### 1:20-1:40 - Explain What Judges Are Seeing

Visual: side-by-side of live RSSI values and estimated position.

Voiceover:
"The key idea is data over geometry. Indoor Wi-Fi signals bounce off walls, doors, glass, and people. RmFindr treats those distortions as useful location signatures."

### 1:40-1:55 - Why It Matters

Visual: quick montage or slide-style overlays: mall, hospital, campus, station, airport.

Voiceover:
"This could help people find shops, gates, clinic rooms, lecture halls, and office suites without installing expensive dedicated beacon hardware."

### 1:55-2:00 - Close

Visual: final dashboard state with map, anchors, calibration points, and live dot.

Voiceover:
"RmFindr makes indoor spaces searchable, navigable, and measurable using the signals already in the air."

## Editing Notes

- Show the live dashboard by 0:10 or earlier.
- Keep the product name visible at the start and end.
- Use captions for the three-step loop: calibrate, scan, estimate.
- Do not spend the demo video explaining every backend component. Save that for the technical walkthrough.
- Best final shot: the live dot, anchors, calibration points, trail, and confidence/status indicator all visible together.
